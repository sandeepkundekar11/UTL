Andhra Pradesh - E-Seva Centre Project (G2C and B2C Services)

First of its kind service in India directly connecting citizens with the Government.

  • Contract was awarded to UTL in 2003
  • Set up 29 e-Seva centres (with 290 service counters)
  • 5 Lakhs transactions per month across these centres
  • Services include: Utility Bill/Tax payments, Certificates, Permits, Licenses, Facilitation services and Information services.

Maharashtra - Transport Sector Solutions (Driving License and Registration)

First of its kind project in India wherein the Driving Licenses are issued in the form of Smart Cards which are compliant to various domestic and international standards.

  • Contract was awarded to UTL in 2004.
  • Based on the BOO model for 10 years, extendable to 15 years.
  • Issue of smart card based driving license with biometrics (Photograph, Signature and Fingerprints)
  • Processor based SCOSTA compliant Smart Cards with 4KB user memory
  • SARATHI software of NIC for backend database.
  • UTL issues 2 million smart card based driving licenses per annum.

Pondicherry (Transport Solutions - DL and High Security Registration Plates - HSRP)

Driving License and Registration Services

  • Contract was awarded to UTL in 2012.
  • Based on the BOO model for 10 years, extendable to 15 years.
  • Issue of smart card based driving license with biometrics (Photograph, Signature and Fingerprints).
  • Processor based SCOSTA compliant Smart Cards with 4KB user memory
  • High Security Registration Plates (HSRP) are issued throughout Pondicherry which are compliant to various domestic and international standards.

Jharkhand - Common Service Centre (CSC) project (G2C and B2C Services)

Major initiative towards the ambitious plan of the Government of India to set-up 100,000 CSCs across India

  • Contract was awarded to UTL in 2007.
  • Based on the BOOT model for 4 years.
  • Set up 2943 Common Service Centres (CSCs) in the districts of Ranchi, Hazaribagh and Kolhan.
  • Each CSC has a networked kiosk offering various G2C and B2C services - run by a village level entrepreneur.
  • Services envisaged include e-government services, education, health & tele-medicine, financial, entertainment and others
